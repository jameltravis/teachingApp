Syllabus

Fall 2017 Psychology 102

Course Description

This course is designed to provide students with an introduction to different concepts, theories, and applications of psychology. Topics include: goals and theories of psychology, experimental method, biology and behavior, memory, cognition, language and intelligence, psychological disorders, learning theories, behavioral therapies, social cognition, conformity, obedience and compliance.

Required Text

Meyers, D.G. (2013). Psychology (10th Ed.). New York, NY: Worth Publishers.

Expectations

Attendance

Regular attendance is expected; please get to class on time. There will be a lot of material covered in this class. In order to ensure you obtain and process all information given, attending class is crucial. Attendance will be taken at the beginning of every class session. If you miss class you are responsible the material covered during that class. In case of absence, please notify me as soon as possible and provide documentation when appropriate.

Class Participation

Please be prepared! The lectures will cover the majority of the material; however, reading prior to the lecture will ensure that you will be better equipped to participate in classroom discussions, thus enhancing the learning process.

General Information and Policies

Office Hours

I am available to talk and answer any questions regarding the material after class. If possible, email me your questions ahead of time so that I am better prepared to answer your question when we meet.

Lecture Notes

Important material will be presented in lecture. This means that if you must miss class, you should contact a classmate and request their notes. Under no circumstances will I make my lecture notes available to students.

Class Participation and Conduct

I greatly look forward to exploring different avenues of psychology with. The classroom is an environment where all students should feel free to express their ideas in an appropriate manner. Please respect the views and opinions of your fellow classmates. Cell phones will not be allowed in the class. Please turn your cell phone off when entering the class. Text messaging will not be tolerated. With respect to your fellow classmates, refrain from side conversations/chatter in the classroom. If you have a question, feel free to ask me.

Accommodations

CUNY York College is committed to providing access to programs and services for qualified students with disabilities. If you are a student with a disability and require accommodations to participate and complete requirements for this class, contact the STAR Program (Specialized Testing and Academic Resources), services for students with disabilities (Academic Core Building (AC), Room 1GO2) for verification of eligibility and determination of specific accommodations.

Grading

Exams

There are 7 exams in this course. The exams are based on material covered in class and from the readings. Course material covered in one exam will not reappear in subsequent exams. Tests (Including final) are NOT cumulative. The exams will be used to test your knowledge of the material presented in lecture and the required readings. Each exam will consist of a mixture of multiple choice, true/false questions and 2-3 essay questions. NO MAKE-UP EXAMS.

Presentations

Each student will present 2 five minute presentations on one of 7 assigned topics. For these presentations, students are expected to write a 1-2 page summary of their topic, what was learned, and how might this new knowledge be applied.

Writing Assignment

There will be a single 4-5 page paper assigned to assess your writing competency and your understanding of broader psychological concepts. This assignment should conform to APA formatting.

Grading Breakdown

  • Exams - 70%
  • Writing Assignment - 15%
  • Presentation - 10%
  • Participation - 5%
